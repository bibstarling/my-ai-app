# Anti-AI Detection Guide

## ‚ùå AI Detection Red Flags We AVOID

### Generic Resume/Cover Letter Phrases
**AI typically uses:**
- "I am writing to express my strong interest in..."
- "Results-driven professional with a proven track record..."
- "Dynamic leader with extensive experience..."
- "I am confident that my skills align perfectly with..."
- "Thank you for considering my application. I look forward to hearing from you."

**We use instead:**
- Direct, conversational openings
- Specific details about actual work
- Natural enthusiasm without formality
- Real examples, not buzzwords

### Overly Polished Language
**AI typically writes:**
- Perfect, formal sentences with no contractions
- Corporate jargon and buzzword soup
- Repetitive sentence structures
- No personality or voice variation

**We write instead:**
- Natural contractions (I'm, I've, that's, here's)
- Varied sentence lengths‚Äîshort punchy ones. And longer, detailed ones that flow naturally.
- Conversational but professional tone
- Personality and energy that feels real

### Generic Enthusiasm
**AI typically says:**
- "I am excited about this opportunity"
- "I would be honored to contribute"
- "I am passionate about [generic thing]"

**We say instead:**
- Specific excitement about actual company challenges
- Genuine connections to the work
- Matter-of-fact confidence about capabilities

## üéØ Key Principles

### What Makes It Sound Human:

1. **Contractions**: Real people use them. Always.
2. **Varied rhythm**: Mix short sentences. With longer ones that flow naturally and provide detail.
3. **Specificity**: Real project names, real numbers, real challenges
4. **Personality**: Energy, confidence, slight informality
5. **Active voice**: "I ship products" not "products were shipped"
6. **Natural connections**: Company research feels genuine, not forced
7. **Mini-stories**: "When X happened, I did Y, result was Z"
8. **Dashes for emphasis**‚Äîlike this‚Äîit's conversational
9. **Real enthusiasm**: About specific things, not generic "opportunities"
10. **Confident but not corporate**: Matter-of-fact about capabilities

### What AI Typically Does (That We Avoid):

1. ‚ùå No contractions - sounds stiff
2. ‚ùå Repetitive sentence structures - sounds robotic
3. ‚ùå Generic buzzwords - sounds like everyone else
4. ‚ùå Overly formal - sounds like it's from 1995
5. ‚ùå Perfect grammar without flow - sounds unnatural
6. ‚ùå Generic enthusiasm - sounds fake
7. ‚ùå Corporate jargon soup - sounds like a press release
8. ‚ùå Passive voice - sounds weak
9. ‚ùå Formulaic structure - sounds templated
10. ‚ùå No personality - sounds like a bot

## üß™ Self-Test for Human-Sounding Content

Read your resume or cover letter out loud. Ask yourself:

- ‚úÖ Would I actually say this to someone?
- ‚úÖ Does it sound like me talking?
- ‚úÖ Are there specific details only I would know?
- ‚úÖ Does it have energy and personality?
- ‚úÖ Am I using contractions naturally?
- ‚úÖ Do sentence lengths vary?
- ‚úÖ Does it feel conversational but professional?

If you answered "no" to any of these, it might sound AI-generated.

## üí° Pro Tips

1. **Read it out loud**: If it sounds stiff or formal when spoken, it sounds AI-generated
2. **Count contractions**: Humans use 3-5 per paragraph. AI often uses zero.
3. **Check sentence starts**: If every sentence starts with "I" or the same word, it's robotic
4. **Look for specifics**: Generic phrases = AI. Specific details = human.
5. **Feel the energy**: Human writing has rhythm and flow. AI writing feels flat.
6. **Genuine connections**: Your interest in the company should feel real, not researched
